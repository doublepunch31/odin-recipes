Beshbarmak Recipe

Description

Beshbarmak, meaning "five fingers" in Kazakh and Kyrgyz, is a traditional Central Asian dish named after the custom of eating it with your hands. This hearty meal consists of tender boiled meat,traditionally horse meat, but often lamb or beef, served over wide, flat noodles and topped with caramelized onions in a rich meat broth. The dish is a symbol of hospitality and celebration, typically prepared for honored guests and special occasions, with the host often presenting the best cuts of meat to the most respected guests at the table.

Ingredients

  • Lamb, beef, or horse meat (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • Onions, thinly sliced
  • Carrots, whole or halved
  • Bay leaves
  • Black peppercorns
  • Salt
  • Fresh dill or parsley
  • Flour (for noodles)
  • Eggs (for noodles)
  • Water

Steps

  1. Place meat in a large pot, cover with cold water, and bring to a boil
  2. Skim off foam, then add whole onions, carrots, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt
  3. Simmer on low heat for 2-3 hours until meat is very tender
  4. While meat cooks, make noodle dough by mixing flour, eggs, salt, and water
  5. Knead dough until smooth, cover, and let rest for 30 minutes
  6. Roll out dough very thin and cut into wide squares or rectangles
  7. Remove cooked meat and vegetables from broth, strain broth
  8. Boil noodles in the broth for 5-7 minutes until tender
  9. In a separate pan, fry thinly sliced onions in some broth until soft and golden
  10. Slice or shred the meat into bite-sized pieces
  11. Arrange noodles on a large platter, top with meat and caramelized onions
  12. Pour some hot broth over the dish and garnish with fresh herbs
  13. Serve with extra broth on the side for dipping
