Plov Recipe

Description

Plov is Central Asia's beloved national dish, a fragrant rice pilaf cooked in large cast-iron pots called kazans. The dish combines long-grain rice with lamb, carrots, onions, and garlic, seasoned with cumin and cooked in plenty of oil. The technique involves browning the meat and vegetables first, then layering rice on top to steam and absorb all the flavors. More than just food, plov is a symbol of hospitality and celebration, often prepared in huge quantities for weddings and community gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Long-grain rice
  • Lamb or beef, cut into chunks
  • Carrots, julienned
  • Onions, sliced
  • Garlic, whole heads
  • Vegetable oil or lamb fat
  • Cumin
  • Salt and pepper
  • Barberries or raisins (optional)
  • Hot water or broth

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large kazan or heavy pot over high heat
  2. Brown the meat chunks until golden on all sides
  3. Add sliced onions and cook until soft and translucent
  4. Add julienned carrots and stir-fry until slightly caramelized
  5. Season with cumin, salt, and pepper
  6. Pour in hot water or broth to cover the ingredients and simmer for 30-40 minutes
  7. Rinse rice thoroughly until water runs clear
  8. Layer the rice evenly over the meat and vegetables without stirring
  9. Push whole garlic heads into the rice
  10. Add more hot water to cover the rice by about 1 inch
  11. Cook on high heat until water is absorbed
  12. Reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and steam for 20-30 minutes
  13. Turn off heat and let rest for 10 minutes before serving
